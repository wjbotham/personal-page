April/May 2024 Media

Television

  • Resident Alien (S1-2). I'm not into cringe comedy so Alan Tudyk needs to dial it back like 30% but otherwise this is a shockingly good show. I came for literally anything to do with Alice Wetterlund and I stayed for beautiful moments about friends building a community life together and Corey Reynolds' incredible nonsense monologues. I will steal season 3 sometime soon.
  • Star Trek: Discovery (S5). It was alright. Didn't blow me out of the water like you ought to if you're pulling out the Progenitors, but the alien first contact at the end of season 4 was so cool and weird that I would feel greedy if I complained about this now. I liked that they ended with Michael being a cool old lady in a nice house with a family. That's my kind of fanservice.

Books

  • Blindsight (Peter Watts). A lot of the horror of this story missed me because I've been jaded for a long time already about the idea that I'm a whole conscious entity. It was a trip reading it now for the first time because non-conscious algorithms that produce convincing human language without understanding are a real danger that I have to dodge at my actual day job in 2024. My unanswered discussion question is: Why are people are attached to conscious awareness as an essential human trait, and why are they unsettled by the thought of not having it?
  • The Other Significant Others (Rhaina Cohen). A very timely read as I'm currently having a mild early midlife crisis about whether or not I'll have meaningful long-term relationships without getting married and having kids. This book didn't give me advice or, you know, hope, but I guess I would heartily recommend it as an informative overview of the subject.
