June/August 2024 Media

Movies

  • Nope. I don't care for horror unless it has big ideas about the human need to be at the center of the universe. God I hope they never make a sequel.
  • Tenet. A bad spy movie with weird pacing but a very cool time travel movie?

Television

  • Counterpart. A very cool spy show about a cold war between our world and a parallel universe that was identical until 30 years ago. The main character is a meek bureaucrat and his alternate is a hardened spy; I expected it to go in a direction where they each succumb to temptations to become more like each other but they both end up being stuck in their ways, more of a meditation on grieving what could have been.
  • The Owl House (S1). Super charming. Cool wizard duels. Relatable tired old Xennial lady.

Books

  • The Ballad of Black Tom (Victor LaVelle). More brutal than I can usually stomach but a great story that does more with cosmic horror than terrified baby H.P. Lovecraft ever did.
  • Victories Greater Than Death (Charlie Jane Anders). More YA than I was expecting but a decent queer space opera story. It has the best explanation I've heard for the prevalence of humanoid aliens: Because the first spacefaring civilization were humanoid chauvinists and destroyed all the non-humanoid species they could find.
